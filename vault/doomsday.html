What is the Doomsday Algorithm?

The Doomsday Algorithm is a mental technique for calculating the day of the week for any given date. It works by using so-called "Doomsday" dates that fall on the same weekday every year, and a few easy-to-remember rules to adjust for the year and month. By memorizing these anchor points and doing some quick arithmetic, you can figure out any date‚Äôs weekday without a calendar.

Century Anchor Days

For each century, the Doomsday falls on a specific weekday (known as the anchor day for that century). The Gregorian calendar repeats every 400 years, so there are only four anchor days to remember:

  • 1800s: Friday (weekday #5)
  • 1900s: Wednesday (weekday #3)
  • 2000s: Tuesday (weekday #2)
  • 2100s: Sunday (weekday #0)

Knowing the century‚Äôs anchor day lets you start the calculation for any year in that century.

Steps to Calculate the Weekday

  1. Find the century‚Äôs anchor day: Use the list above to get the anchor weekday for the target date‚Äôs century.
  2. Calculate the year‚Äôs Doomsday: Using the last two digits of the year, calculate that year‚Äôs own ‚Äúdoomsday‚Äù weekday. (There is a formula: divide by 12, take remainder, divide remainder by 4, and add them up with the century anchor, mod 7 ‚Äì or use a memorized table.) This gives the weekday for all the anchor dates in that year.
  3. Find the nearest Doomsday date: From the target date‚Äôs month, pick the known doomsday date (see table above). This will have the same weekday as the year‚Äôs doomsday.
  4. Count to the target date: Calculate the difference in days between the known doomsday date and the target date. Move forward or backward that many days from the known weekday.
  5. Adjust for January/February of leap years: If your date is in Jan or Feb *and* the year is a leap year, subtract one day from your final count (since in leap years the doomsday shifts one day earlier for those months).
